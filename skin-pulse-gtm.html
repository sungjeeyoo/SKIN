SKIN OS · Pulse Launch

Six-week test
framework

Three sequential phases with hard kill criteria at every gate — channel selection, CTA split testing, and scale decision.

Phase hypothesis
One channel will surface meaningfully more qualified founders with lower friction than the others. The goal is to find it in 2 weeks — not to optimize it.

Run these 4 micro-tests in parallel (2 weeks)

A · SKIN OS organic
Add a persistent banner or interstitial inside SKIN OS directing existing visitors to the Snapshot. No new traffic needed — test what existing intent converts to.
Metric: Snapshot starts / OS visitors Effort: Low Signal: ≥8% start rate = valid
B · LinkedIn DM outbound
Send 30–50 personalised DMs to pre-seed / seed founders (recent raise announcements = best timing signal). Offer the Snapshot as a free diagnostic. Track opens → starts.
Metric: Starts / DMs sent Effort: Medium Signal: ≥15% start rate = valid
C · Investor partner drop
Ask 2–3 investors (Dominik + network) to forward the Snapshot to 5–10 portfolio founders each. Frame as a portfolio resource, not a product pitch. Warm referral removes friction.
Metric: Starts / referred founders Effort: Low (once set up) Signal: ≥25% start rate = valid
D · Community post (Slack / WhatsApp)
Post in 2–3 founder Slack groups or WhatsApp communities where you already have credibility. Anchor on a specific, timely problem (e.g. "pre-fundraise health check"). Track clicks → starts.
Metric: Starts / post reach Effort: Low Signal: ≥5% start rate = valid
Phase 1 decision gate — end of week 2
KeepAny channel hitting its signal threshold. Carry forward top 1–2 into Phase 2.
KillAny channel below threshold after 2 weeks — don't invest further time in it.
PivotIf zero channels hit threshold: the bottleneck is the Snapshot's perceived value, not distribution. Revisit the free offer before retesting channels.

Phase hypothesis
On the winning channel, one CTA will generate a meaningfully higher signal of downstream value than the others. The $99 upgrade rate is the north star — the other two are proxies that tell you where conversion intent sits.

Test 3 CTAs on the winning channel (2 weeks)

CTA 1 · Upgrade to full Pulse ($99)
Direct hard CTA at the end of the Snapshot — "Get the full human-reviewed report for $99." This is the money signal: if this converts, you have product-market fit for the paid tier, not just the free one.
North star: Upgrade rate Threshold: ≥3% of Snapshot completions
CTA 2 · Email capture (Pulse waitlist)
Soft CTA: "We'll send you the full breakdown when it's ready." Tests whether founders see enough value to trade an email but aren't ready to pay. High conversion here = demand, not monetisation intent.
Metric: Email capture rate Threshold: ≥20% of completions
CTA 3 · Book a 30-min founder call
High-friction CTA: "Want to walk through your results with someone who's seen hundreds of decks?" Tests willingness to commit time (high-intent signal) and surfaces Fractional Program candidates.
Metric: Call booking rate Threshold: ≥5% of completions
Reading the results
High CTA 1 = paid conversion path is working. Scale. High CTA 2 only = demand without willingness to pay — Snapshot needs a stronger value wedge before the $99 ask. High CTA 3 = you're generating Fractional leads, not Pulse leads — treat those as a separate track.
Phase 2 decision gate — end of week 4
ScaleCTA 1 hits ≥3% — commit to the channel + CTA combo, invest in more volume.
NurtureCTA 1 sub-threshold but CTA 2 strong — build an email nurture sequence from captures into the $99 offer. Don't expect direct Snapshot-to-Pulse conversion yet.
ReframeAll CTAs sub-threshold — the Snapshot isn't landing as a decision tool. The format, framing, or delivery of the free product needs a change before you scale distribution.
Wk 1Wk 2Wk 3Wk 4Wk 5Wk 6
Phase hypothesis
The winning channel + CTA combo can be systematically amplified without degrading conversion quality. Week 5–6 proves whether this is a repeatable motion or a one-time signal.

Scale decision — choose one path

Path S1 · Amplify the same channel
Double volume on the winning channel. If LinkedIn worked, send 100 DMs instead of 40. If investor referral worked, add 2 more investors. Watch whether conversion rate holds as volume increases.
Watch: Conversion rate at 2x volume Pass: Rate holds within 30% of Phase 2 rate
Path S2 · Add the second-best channel
Keep the primary channel running and turn on the next-best Phase 1 result. Tests whether the conversion combo holds across distribution contexts — or if it was audience-specific.
Watch: New channel conversion vs baseline Pass: Within 50% of primary channel rate
Path S3 · Embed in a partner's existing flow
Approach one accelerator, pre-seed fund, or founder community to offer the Snapshot as a standard onboarding resource. Single integration point → ongoing pipeline with no recurring distribution effort.
Watch: Monthly Snapshot volume from partner Pass: ≥20 completions / month from one partner
End-of-test scorecard — week 6
ChannelWhich channel(s) produced qualified starts consistently at acceptable cost-per-start.
CTAWhich conversion goal had the strongest signal — and whether it maps to Pulse revenue or Fractional pipeline.
CAC proxyEstimated time/cost to generate one Snapshot completion. Benchmark against $99 Pulse revenue to assess payback.
QualitativeWhat do completers say? Do they feel they got value from the Snapshot alone — or do they want more?

Why this sequence

Channel before CTA
Testing CTAs on a weak channel will give you false negatives. The sequence forces you to find qualified audience first, then optimise conversion — not both at once.
Kill fast, not slow
2-week windows with hard thresholds prevent you from over-investing in a channel that feels promising but isn't converting. The kill criteria are not aspirational — they're the minimum signal that justifies continued attention.
Conversion goal ≠ business goal
Email capture is easy to hit but doesn't validate the $99 Pulse. Call bookings are hard to hit but are the strongest Fractional signal. You're testing three different things — each CTA is a proxy for a different revenue path.
Free product must feel complete
The Snapshot can't be a "teaser" — founders will sense it immediately and disengage. It needs to deliver a genuine insight before the upgrade CTA appears. The difference between a useful subset and a demo is the moment of micro-clarity.
What you're not testing here
Pricing — $99 is fixed for now. Price sensitivity is a Phase 2+ problem, not a launch problem.
Positioning copy — keep the framing consistent across channels so results are comparable. Vary the distribution, not the message.
Product format — the Snapshot is a subset of the Pulse report. Don't redesign it mid-test. Evaluate format after the GTM test completes.
